Welcome to our knitting calculators

Whether you're a seasoned knitting pro or just picking up your first set of needles, we've got you covered. Our knitting calculator is the perfect tool to help you create beautiful, custom-made knitted creations with ease and precision.

Knitting calculations are essential for creating well-fitting and properly proportioned knitwear, whether you're following a pattern or designing your own. Here are some of the most common knitting calculations and how they are used:

Gauge: Gauge is the number of stitches and rows per inch or centimeter that you achieve when knitting with a particular yarn and needle size. To calculate gauge, you typically cast on a certain number of stitches, work for a set number of rows, and then measure the resulting fabric. Accurate gauge is crucial for ensuring that your finished project matches the intended size in a pattern.

Stitch Count: You might need to calculate the number of stitches to cast on for a project, which depends on your gauge and the desired measurements. To do this, multiply your desired width by your gauge in stitches per inch (or centimeter).

Row Count: Similarly, you might need to calculate the number of rows to knit for a specific section of your project. This involves multiplying the desired length by your gauge in rows per inch (or centimeter). Increases and Decreases: When shaping a project, such as creating a waistline in a sweater, you need to calculate how to evenly distribute increases or decreases. For instance, if you're increasing stitches evenly across a row, divide the total number of stitches to be increased by the number of increases needed, and then space them accordingly.

Yarn Length: To determine how much yarn you need for a project, you'll need to calculate the yardage required based on the yarn weight and the number of stitches and rows. Patterns usually provide information on how many yards are required for a specific size, but you can also calculate it based on your gauge and measurements.

Yarn Substitution: If you want to use a different yarn than what's specified in a pattern, you'll need to calculate how it will affect your gauge and make necessary adjustments to ensure the finished project's size matches the pattern.
